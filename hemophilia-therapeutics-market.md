<p><p>The hemophilia therapeutics market is segmented into three main types: Hemophilia A, Hemophilia B, and Hemophilia C. Hemophilia A accounts for the majority of cases and primarily involves factor VIII deficiency, necessitating factor replacement therapies. Hemophilia B, characterized by factor IX deficiency, requires similar treatments but is less common. Hemophilia C, associated with factor XI deficiency, generally presents milder symptoms and is often treated with desmopressin or factor replacement. Each segment has unique treatment options and evolving market dynamics.</p></p>
