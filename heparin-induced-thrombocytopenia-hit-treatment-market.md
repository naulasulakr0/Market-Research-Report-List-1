<p><p>Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia (HIT) treatment market includes various diagnostic tests to identify this serious condition. Enzyme Immunoassay (EIA) detects antibodies against heparin-platelet factor complexes. The Serotonin Release Assay (SRA) assesses platelet activation in the presence of heparin. Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA) quantifies specific antibodies, while the Heparin-Induced Platelet Activation (HIPA) test evaluates platelet response to heparin. These tests are crucial for diagnosing HIT and guiding appropriate treatment strategies, impacting patient management and outcomes significantly.</p></p>
