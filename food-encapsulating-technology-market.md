<p><p>Food encapsulating technology involves various methods for enclosing food ingredients within a protective coating, enhancing their stability, delivery, and functionality. Microencapsulation targets larger particles, typically in the micrometer range, providing controlled release and protection from environmental factors. Nanoencapsulation, on the other hand, deals with much smaller particles, enhancing bioavailability and allowing for targeted delivery within the body. Other market types include polymer-based encapsulation and edible coatings, which focus on improving shelf life and sensory attributes of food products.</p></p>
