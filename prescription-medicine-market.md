<p><p>The prescription medicine market is divided into two main types: original brand drugs and generic drugs. Original brand drugs are developed by pharmaceutical companies and marketed under a specific brand name, often protected by patents, which allows them to recoup research and development costs. Generic drugs, on the other hand, contain the same active ingredients as brand-name counterparts but are sold under their chemical name after the original patent expires, typically at lower prices, making them more accessible to consumers.</p></p>
