<p><p>The EDLC (Electric Double Layer Capacitor) supercapacitor market is primarily divided into two types: double-layer capacitors and pseudocapacitors. Double-layer capacitors store energy through the electrostatic separation of charge at the electrode-electrolyte interface, offering high power density and rapid charge/discharge capabilities. Pseudocapacitors, on the other hand, utilize fast redox reactions at the electrode surface, providing higher energy density compared to double-layer capacitors. These technologies cater to diverse applications, from energy storage systems to electronics, driven by their unique performance characteristics.</p></p>
