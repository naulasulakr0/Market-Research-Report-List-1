<p><p>The intrauterine contraceptive market comprises two main types: nonhormonal and hormonal intrauterine contraceptive devices (IUDs). Nonhormonal IUDs, typically made of copper, prevent fertilization and may create an unfavorable environment for sperm. Hormonal IUDs release progestin, thickening cervical mucus and thinning the uterine lining to inhibit pregnancy. Both types offer long-term, effective contraceptive options, but cater to different preferences and medical needs, influencing their usage dynamics within the family planning landscape.</p></p>
