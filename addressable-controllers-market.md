<p><p>Addressable controllers in the market can be classified into two main types: wired and wireless. Wired addressable controllers use physical connections, such as cables, to communicate with connected devices, offering reliability and consistent performance. In contrast, wireless addressable controllers utilize radio frequencies or other wireless technologies for communication, providing flexibility and easier installation in environments where running cables is challenging. Both types cater to various applications, ensuring efficient control and monitoring in smart building and automation systems.</p></p>
