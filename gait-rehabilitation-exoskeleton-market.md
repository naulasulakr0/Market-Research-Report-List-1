<p><p>The gait rehabilitation exoskeleton market is categorized into single joint and multiple joint types. Single joint exoskeletons target specific areas, such as the knee or ankle, allowing for precise rehabilitation and support during walking. In contrast, multiple joint exoskeletons facilitate movement across various joints, offering comprehensive support for the entire lower limb. This versatility enables more natural gait patterns and broader rehabilitation applications, catering to a wider range of patients with varying mobility challenges.</p></p>
