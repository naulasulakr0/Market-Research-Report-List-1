<p><p>The antifungal infection therapeutics market encompasses several drug classes. Polyenes, such as amphotericin B, disrupt fungal cell membranes. Echinocandins inhibit cell wall synthesis, making them effective against invasive fungal infections. Azoles, including fluconazole, target ergosterol synthesis, altering membrane integrity. Allylamines, like terbinafine, block fungal squalene epoxidase, preventing cell growth. Other drug types include various novel agents and combination therapies, contributing to diverse treatment options for combating fungal infections and addressing emerging resistance challenges in clinical settings.</p></p>
