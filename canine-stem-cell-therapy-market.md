<p><p>Canine stem cell therapy market primarily consists of two types: allogeneic and autologous stem cells. Allogeneic stem cells are derived from a donor dog, offering potential benefits like broader genetic variability and ease of use for treatment. In contrast, autologous stem cells are harvested directly from the individual dog, ensuring compatibility and reducing rejection risks. Both types aim to promote healing and regeneration in various conditions, but their sourcing methods cater to different therapeutic needs and patient circumstances.</p></p>
